2 at a Time

As Founder & CEO of Impart, I recruited a stellar technical team, launched multiple web applications with paying customers, and ran a profitable business in our first full year.

How? If I had to choose a one-word answer: focus.

One of my trusted Impart advisers was a serial entrepreneur. He had multiple successful exits and raised multiple VC rounds. He offered the most important advice of my career:

Focus. And focus on 2 goals at a time.

startup's freedom to pursue anything is a double-edged sword. I see many startups fail because they pursue too much all at once. Successful startups wholly focus on perfect execution of a few top priorities.

To be clear, many pursuits - focused or not - will fail. But at least you fail fast, fail with certainty, and quickly move towards success. Remember, Ideas are cheap. Execution is everything.

Why 2 not 1 goal? If you're really in it, you're probably obsessing over your startup like your own baby. Often times you need step away to see the best solution. The other goal gives you that break, and vice versa.

Examples from Impart:

  • Year 1: MVP
    • Find Product-Market Fit
    • Sell Before I Build
  • Year 2: Prototype
    • Recruit a Stellar Technical Team
    • Launch a Scalable Product Users Love
